Classical or Acoustic Guitar ?

There are many diferences between
classical and acoustic guitars.
I am going to show you the main differences and some helpful informations.

.

Let's Go Through Them !

.

The Classical Guitar

  • This in a tipical classical guitar. The main diference between the classical guitar and other guitars is that this one's strings are made out of nylon.
  • Classical guitars have smaller bodies and wider necks then standard acoustic guitars.
    They tipically have slotted headstocks with poles that the strings are wrapped around.
  • Classical guitars are usually easier to learn
    for beginners, because the string material is
    softer than steel strings that the acoustic
    guitars use.
  • The fretboard of a classical guitar is a lot wider than that of an acoustic. Quite often classical
    guitars will not have the fret markers (dots or inlays) along the fingerboard either.
  • Often classical guitars are a little cheaper than their acoustic cousins, which is why many
    beginners start with a classical guitar first.
  • If you are thinking about starting out, you should buy a classical guitar as your first one.

The Acoustic Guitar

  • Acoustic guitars predominantly come in a
    dreadnought shape wich is considerably larger
    than that of a classical guitar and cutaways
    where you can access the higher frets.
  • On classical guitars these are rather rare.
  • A classical guitar uses nylon strings whereas the modern acoustic uses steel strings, hence it's
    often referred to as a "steel string acoustic".
  • With steel-string acoustic guitar strings you get a very twangy and bright sound that resonates
    (lasts longer) than a classical guitar. They also feel very different too.
  • Steel-string acoustic guitars use a variety of metals for strings, including nickel and bronze
    and they are closer to the likes of electric guitar strings i.e thinner and somewhat sharper on
    fingers.

To Note :

  • First of all, let's clear up one thing - both these guitars are in fact acoustic guitars, except one
    uses nylon strings (classical) and the other uses steel strings (acoustic). Confusing, yes but the
    differences between the two are vast.

So, which one is the right
guitar for me ?

  • If you like the sound of Flamenco or Spanish guitar, want a cheaper alternative to learn the
    basics with a lighter option that is slightly easier
    to carry, the classical guitar might be for you.
